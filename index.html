My Work Process

Everything is born from a single idea. They can range from mundane to fantastical, but they should all be centered around actions that the player can do in the game and emotions the player should feel while playing. My first step is to draw out a system. What does the player first see when they start the game? How does the player's interest change over time? Then comes prototyping and experimentation. Continuing to iterate designs and scaffold upon previous prototypes to create something even more interesting is key to creating a good game. Finally, I polish and add additional features to emphasize the most engaging mechanics of the game. Just like that, the game is (hopefully) complete!

But what is the game is... semi-complete? Well of course, not every game turns out the way you want it to. If my game turns out to be an unplayable mess, then I simply write a depressing postmortem and move on! Then perhaps one day, the knowledge gained from that failure will be used to create something even more spectacular!
